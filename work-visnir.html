VIS-NIR SPECTROMETER TESTING

Spectroscopy R&D

OBJECTIVE

To create a cost-effective, non-destructive method of assessing Hass avocado maturity using a portable spectrometer prototype. By identifying optimal wavelengths, the research aims to overcome the limitations of conventional postharvest quality assessment methods.

PROCESS

Mature avocados were individually sampled and stored to analyze spectral data and develop respective predictive models over time. Wavelength selection involved one-way ANOVA and a Tukey HSD post hoc test to identify sensitive bands for the spectrometer prototype.

RESULTS

The study confirmed that lights in the 890 nm to 930 nm NIR region are statistically more sensitive to physiological changes when measuring the reflectance of ripening avocados with Vis-NIR spectroscopy. The results provide more specific and effective parameters for measuring avocado maturity, aiding further investigations in a nondestructive, cost-effective, and rapid postharvest avocado assessment system.
