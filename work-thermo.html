THERMOSTAT LOGIC FRAMEWORK

Digital Logic Design

OBJECTIVE

To develop a thermostat logic framework with user override capabilities, aligning with diverse stakeholder preferences. The primary aim is to choose a cost-effective and space-efficient logic design.

PROCESS

Two synchronous sequential machine designs (Design 1 and Design 2) are proposed, incorporating adjustments based on stakeholder inputs. Boolean equations, Karnaugh maps, and logic gate details are derived for both designs. The final design is selected through a systematic criteria evaluation via the Pairwise Comparison Matrix and the Pugh Decision Matrix.

RESULTS

Design 1 emerges as the preferred choice due to its lower number of logic gates, resulting in cost and size advantages. Both designs share the same number of flip-flops, yet Design 1 offers more design clarity, particularly concerning input 11.
